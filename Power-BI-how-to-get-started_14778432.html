The structure

In power BI you can add content like datasets, reports, dashboards and excel files which can be viewed by other users via mobile or browser. This is all managed online in the Power BI service and can be accessed from here. The grouping of content as mentioned is organized using workspaces. The content can be developed using power bi desktop an application locally installed. Finally the created datasets can be analyzed using an excel plugin:

When entering the service you will arrive at the home page:

Power BI Workspaces

Workspaces are places to collaborate with colleagues on specific content. Workspaces are created by Power BI designers to hold collections of dashboards and reports. The designer can then share the workspace with colleagues. Designers can also bundle a collection of dashboards and reports into an app and distribute it to the entire community, to their organization, or to specific people or groups. Certain types of apps, called template apps, create a workspace when the app is installed. Learn more about apps.

Everyone using the Power BI service also has a My workspace. My workspace is your personal sandbox where you can create content for yourself.

Now to find content, you need access to a workspace. If you click on workspaces you should see a list of workspaces which you have granted access to:

In a workspace content is shown like this (example of CD Finance workspace):

There are two types of workspaces. Premium workspace which can be recognized by the diamond after the name, and the normal workspace. For a normal workspace you need a pro license to access.

Power BI Apps:

Power BI designers can create official packaged content, then distribute it to a broad audience as an app. Apps are created in workspaces, where members can collaborate on Power BI content with their colleagues before publishing the finished app to individuals, large groups of people in an organization, or an entire organization. By using an app, it's easier for designers to manage permissions on these collections since the app can have permissions that are different than those set on the workspace.

Mobile

If you download the power bi app from the store on your mobile device, and you login using the power bi user login, it is possible to view your reports on your mobile device as well.

more information: https://docs.microsoft.com/en-us/power-bi/consumer/mobile/mobile-apps-for-mobile-devices

Central data models

There are multiple ways in power bi to develop data and reports. Different approaches with pro’s and con’s. A central data model is a data model developed for multiple purposes or departments and managed by a central team. This model is available in a workspace, and other users can re-use that model for their own report as a self service.

Develop content

To develop content a person needs power bi desktop, a connection to data and the right on a workspace to publish content (in case you want to share). Find more information in the starters guide.

License model

IHC has acquired Power BI Premium. Therefore two type of licenses are available.

  • Viewer license

  • Power BI Pro license

Viewer license

The viewer license is free of charge and applicable for people who only need view access on apps or workspaces in premium capacity. For more details about the possibilities of the free license please visit the Microsoft web pages: https://docs.microsoft.com/en-us/power-bi/consumer/end-user-features

Pro license

With a pro license you can develop content, and or manage workspaces and accessibility. It’s also possible to collaborate with other pro licensed persons on workspaces not added to the premium capacity. A pro license is not free of charge as there is a monthly amount to be paid per user.

difference of free and pro ; https://docs.microsoft.com/en-us/power-bi/consumer/end-user-license
